## 6. Pricing Strategy Recommendations

### Recommended Model: Hybrid Tiered (Per-User Base + Flat Features)

61% of SaaS companies now use hybrid pricing models, reporting 21% median revenue growth — significantly outperforming pure subscription or pure usage-based models. Given Workflowz serves both small service teams and large manufacturing plants, a hybrid approach is optimal.

### Proposed Tier Structure

#### Free Tier
- **Price**: $0
- **Limits**: Up to 3 users, 2 projects, 500MB storage, basic workflow canvas
- **Purpose**: Acquisition funnel. Benchmark data shows 2-5% freemium conversion with top performers at 8-15%.
- **Competitive positioning**: Matches Monday.com (free for 2 seats), ClickUp (free tier), Zoho Projects (free tier)

#### Starter Tier
- **Price**: $12/user/month (annual) | $15/user/month (monthly)
- **Minimum**: 3 users
- **Includes**: Unlimited projects, workflow canvas, client tracking portal, real-time chat, file uploads (5GB), internal notes, basic CRM (companies, workers, clients), appointment scheduling, 5 client portal users
- **Rationale**: Positioned between Zoho Projects ($5-10/user) and Monday.com Standard ($12/user) — the sweet spot for small service businesses. Includes differentiators (client portal, workflow canvas) that competitors gate behind higher tiers.

#### Professional Tier
- **Price**: $22/user/month (annual) | $27/user/month (monthly)
- **Minimum**: 5 users
- **Includes**: Everything in Starter plus: unlimited client portal users, custom fields, automations (500/month), time tracking, Gantt/timeline view, reporting dashboards, forms/intake, API access, 25GB storage, template library
- **Rationale**: Competes with Monday.com Pro ($19), Asana Advanced ($24.99), Wrike Business ($25), Teamwork Grow ($19.99). This is where most service businesses will land.

#### Business Tier
- **Price**: $35/user/month (annual) | $42/user/month (monthly)
- **Minimum**: 10 users
- **Includes**: Everything in Professional plus: white-labeling (custom domain, branding), advanced reporting/analytics, resource management/workload view, unlimited automations, audit trail, SSO/SAML, custom roles, 100GB storage, priority support, invoicing/billing integration
- **Rationale**: Targets agencies and mid-size businesses. Competes with Teamwork Scale ($54.99), Airtable Business ($45), and undercuts SuiteDash Pinnacle ($99 flat but less PM depth). White-labeling is the killer differentiator here — Monday.com and Asana do not offer this.

#### Enterprise / Manufacturing Tier
- **Price**: Custom (starting ~$50/user/month, with volume discounts)
- **Minimum**: 25+ users
- **Includes**: Everything in Business plus: dedicated instance option, custom integrations, SLA guarantees, compliance features (audit logs, data retention policies), advanced client visibility controls, dedicated CSM, custom onboarding, phone support, HIPAA/SOC2 readiness, unlimited storage
- **Manufacturing Add-on Module** ($200-500/month flat on top of per-user pricing):
  - Production scheduling views
  - Quality checkpoint stages (pass/fail gates in workflow)
  - SLA tracking per stage
  - Barcode/QR integration hooks
  - Custom stage metadata fields (lot numbers, serial numbers, inspection results)
- **Rationale**: For large manufacturing plants, per-user pricing alone is problematic (they may have 100+ workers). Offer volume discounts (e.g., 25-50 users: 15% off, 50-100: 25% off, 100+: 35% off). The manufacturing module as a flat add-on avoids multiplying per-user costs for specialized features only some users need.

### Key Strategy Rationale

**1. Why Per-User (Not Flat Rate) as the Base:**
- Your app has distinct user roles (admin, worker, client). Per-user pricing aligns cost with value — a 5-person team gets less value than a 50-person team.
- 57% of SaaS companies still use per-user pricing. It is simple, predictable, and familiar to buyers.
- Flat rate (like Basecamp at $299/month) works only when you have broad consumer appeal. Your niche benefits from per-user scaling.

**2. Why Free Client Portal Users:**
- Client users are read-only. Charging for them creates friction that prevents adoption of your most differentiating feature.
- Monday.com and Smartsheet both offer unlimited free viewers. This is table stakes.
- On Starter tier, cap at 5 to create upgrade pressure. Professional and above: unlimited.

**3. Why White-Labeling at Business Tier (Not Lower):**
- White-labeling is highly valued by agencies (SuiteDash charges $99/month, Copilot charges $499/month, ManyRequests charges $319/month). It is a premium differentiator.
- Gating it at $35/user/month for a 10-user minimum means $350/month minimum — competitive with SuiteDash ($99 flat but weaker PM) and significantly cheaper than Copilot Advanced ($499).

**4. Manufacturing Volume Pricing:**
- A 100-person manufacturing plant at $50/user/month full price = $5,000/month. With 35% volume discount = $3,250/month. Plus manufacturing add-on at $500/month = $3,750/month total.
- This is dramatically cheaper than Plex ($500/user/month + $100K implementation) or even Katana Professional ($799/month for limited manufacturing features).
- You are not replacing their ERP/MES. You are providing the workflow tracking, client visibility, and communication layer that those tools lack.

**5. Annual vs. Monthly Pricing:**
- Standard 20% discount for annual billing (industry norm is 16-20%).
- The median entry-level SaaS price in 2025 is $29/user/month. Your Starter at $12/user/month is intentionally below this to drive adoption, with expansion revenue coming from upgrades to Professional/Business.

## 7. Unique Competitive Position

Workflowz occupies a niche no single competitor fully covers: **visual workflow canvas + client portal with tracking + CRM + real-time chat + appointment scheduling, all in one platform**. The closest competitors are:

- **Teamwork.com** — PM + client features, but no workflow canvas or white-label
- **SuiteDash** — white-label portal + CRM, but weak project management
- **Copilot/Assembly** — client portal + billing, but no workflow visualization
- **Monday.com** — powerful PM, but limited client portal and no white-label

### Primary Pricing Advantages
1. **Client portal included from the lowest tier** (competitors gate this or charge extra)
2. **White-labeling at a competitive price point** (Business tier, not enterprise-only)
3. **Visual workflow canvas as a core differentiator** (unique among client portal tools)
4. **Manufacturing-friendly volume pricing** (dramatically cheaper than MES/ERP tools for the workflow/tracking layer)

### Top 6 Features to Build Next (by revenue impact)
1. Gantt/timeline view with dependencies
2. Automations/workflow rules
3. Time tracking
4. Reporting dashboards
5. Custom fields
6. Mobile app
